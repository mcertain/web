The User Guide

The place where we explain how all the magic works.

  • CREATE AN ACCOUNT - You will need to create an account so that all of your private data can be stored securely on your device. Nothing is stored in the cloud, even your account profile is stored ONLY on your device. When you link with other devices, your non-private profile details such as your username and profile image are shared with the devices you link to. Please remember that if you decide to delete the app, you will be deleting all of your data including your account profile. So if you reinstall the app later, you will need to create a whole new profile. Your new profile will be seen as a new and unique user and will not be associated with any past messages you have sent. A feature to backup your data so that it can be imported on another device will added in the near future.
  • LOGIN TO YOUR ACCOUNT - Once you have created an account, you can use the password you created to login. If you forget your password, you can press the "Forgot Password" button to display your password hint in the password field. You can also view your password as you type it by pressing the little eyeball icon beside the password field. Press the eyeball icon again to hide your password.
  • SELECT YOUR GROUP NAME - Now that you have an account and you've logged in, click on the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen. In Settings, you need to specify a connection group name like "Friends" or "Family" or "School" for example. WhyChat will use this group name to advertise itself to other nearby users of WhyChat. When it sees another device advertising the same group name, then it will attempt to link with those devices advertising the same group name. Your group name can be changed at anytime and it doesn't impact your message data since your group name is not associated with your private data. Everyone in your group MUST use the same group name, otherwise WhyChat will not be able to find them.
  • CONNECT - You're logged in and your connection group name is set, so now you're FINALLY ready to connect. There are two simple ways to Connect and Disconnect from other WhyChat users. You can either click the button showing "Connect" on your profile page OR you can press the WhyChat connect button from the Connections or Messages tabs. After pressing the connect button you will get an alert confirming that you're ready to join a chat session with the other people in your group.

Red Connection Icon

The red connection icon indicates that you are disconnected from all other devices. This also indicates that you are not even advertising your device for other users to connect to.

Orange Connection Icon

The orange connection icon indicates that you are attempting to connect to the first device advertising the same group name specified in the Settings but so far it hasn't found anyone. This also indicates that you are actively advertising your device for other users to connect to.

Green Connection Icon

The green connection icon indicates that you have connected to 1 or more devices successfully. This also indicates that you are actively advertising your device for other users to connect to. 

  • START CHATTING - Now that you're connected to other devices running WhyChat, you can start chatting just like you would with other messaging applications. You can send normal text messages, photos, file attachments, and Voice Messages. At the top of the Connections and Messages tabs, you will see the connection group name that you specified in Settings.

    The Connections tab lists all of the users you have ever connected to in the past. Users that you are currently connected to show up with green dots beside their username. Orange dots indicate that you're currently attempting to connect to that user's device and a red dots mean that you're no longer connected to that user's device. Clicking on any user will allow you to see their profile details.  

    From the Messages tab, you can send messages to either individuals users or a message to everyone currently connected to your device. Messages sent to all users are not confirmed. The Messages tab also shows you the most recently received message and the time it was received, whether you're connected to that user, their profile photo, and a badge indicating how many unread messages you have from that user.

Composing and Receiving Messages

Composing Text Messages

You can compose text messages just like you normally would in any other messaging application and then hit the send button.

Attaching Photos

When attaching a photo to a message you will want to press the "+" icon, select your photo, crop it if necessary, and then hit the send button.

Attaching Files

Unlike many messaging applications, you can actually send file attachments as well. Any file that you can find in the File Browser on your phone you can send as an attachment.

Recording Voice Messages

When composing a voice message you will tap on the microphone icon first and it should turn red to indicate that you're recording. Once done recording, press the icon again to stop recording and then press send to share with your friends.

Receiving Text Messages

In the received messages section, you will see the messages from you in a dark color and the messages from other users in a light color. Messages not from you will also show the username of the sender above the message. The date of the message will be shown below the message along with the confirmation of whether the messages were: Sent, Delivered, or Read.

Viewing Photos

Received photos will show up in a small preview box highlighted in a dark or light color depending who sent the photo. As with text messages, the date and confirmation will be displayed below the message and the senders username above the message. With photos, you can also make a pinch to zoom gesture on the photo preview to open it in a larger full screen view where you can see the image better.

Viewing File Attachments

File attachments received look very similar to photos but they also have their file name shown below the attachment. Like photos, you can also make a pinch to zoom gesture on the file preview box to open the file in a full screen view so that you can scroll through it more easily. You may also scroll through the file in the file preview box as well. 

Playing Voice Messages

When you receive voice messages the profile icon of the sender will be shown along with the senders name above the messages. The date and confirmation will be shown below the voice message. In addition to Sent, Delivered, and Read status; voice messages will also show a Played status when the receiver plays the message. The waveform representation of the voice audio is also shown along the playback bar just above the elapsed time. The elapsed time increments as the voice is played back.

Failed Messages

When the user you're trying to send a message to goes offline for any reason, then your message will fail to send. When this happens, you can double tap on the message to attempt to resend it once the recipient comes back online.

Saving Photos

Received photos can be saved to any location available in the File Browser or to your Photo album by double tapping or long pressing on or beside the photo shown in the message view.

Saving Files

Received file attachments can be saved to any location available in the File Browser by double tapping or long pressing on or beside the files shown in the message view.

Saving Voice Messages

Like photos and file attachments, received voice messages can be saved to any location available in the File Browser by double tapping or long pressing beside the voice message shown in the message view.
